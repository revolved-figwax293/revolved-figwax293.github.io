## 📈 Understanding your connection

Pingio displays four key metrics. These numbers explain how your internet behaves.

### Download speed
This measures how fast you pull data from the internet. A higher number means you can load webpages and watch videos faster. 

### Upload speed
This measures how fast you send data to the internet. You need good upload speeds for sharing large files or joining video meetings.

### Ping
Ping measures the response time of your connection. Lower numbers are better. High ping causes lag in games and delays during video calls.

### Jitter
Jitter measures the stability of your ping. If your ping varies a lot, your connection quality drops. Stable connections have low jitter.

## ❓ Common questions

**Does the app slow down my internet?**
The app only uses bandwidth during an active test. It stays idle when you are not running a check.

**Can I run it on other operating systems?**
This version works on Windows. 

**Why do my results change?**
Internet speeds fluctuate based on network traffic, time of day, and your connection type (Wi-Fi or Ethernet). If you use Wi-Fi, move closer to your router for better results.

**How do I delete the app?**
Open your Windows Settings. Go to Apps and then Installed Apps. Find Pingio in the list and select Uninstall.

## 💻 Troubleshooting tips

If you encounter issues, try these steps first.

1. Restart the application.
2. Check your physical cables. Ensure your Ethernet cable clicks into your router.
3. Turn off your VPN before running a test. VPNs often lower your speed results.
4. Close other apps that use the internet. Background downloads consume bandwidth and change your test results.

If the app fails to open, ensure you have the correct version of the Windows system runtimes. Most Windows 10 and 11 systems include these by default. Restart your machine if the screen freezes. You can also re-download the file from our link if the installer displays an error.
